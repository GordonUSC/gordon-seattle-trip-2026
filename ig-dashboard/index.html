01 The verdict

You are a connector, not a broadcaster. The data is unanimous on this. You follow out 1.84x more than you are followed back, you give far more likes and comments than you collect, and you send more messages than you receive. On Instagram you are the person who reaches out, shows up, and celebrates other people's joy. Everything below is that instinct, measured. None of it is a grade. It is a map of a life that is unusually generous, plus a few honest notes on where the platform is quietly making money off that generosity, and a handful of small moves that would give more of it back to you.

02 Evolution

Ten years, in messages

2016 was a whisper (34). 2017 you found your feet (883). 2018 you woke up (2,981). 2019 it caught fire (17,272). Then the world shape-shifted: 2020 (7,577), 2021 (9,385), and two roaring peak years, 2022 (29,860) and 2023 (30,308), your loudest year at roughly 83 messages a day. Since then it has softened on purpose: 22,941 in 2024, 13,865 in 2025, about 14k on pace for 2026. Read that curve honestly. The volume that fell is concentrated in high-throughput festival group chats you mostly receive, not your one-to-one threads. Your inner circle largely moved to your 818 iMessage world (377k messages). This is not a community leaving you. It is a nervous system settling into a rhythm it can keep, and your best conversations migrating to rooms that suit them better. You did on instinct what the whole industry is now scrambling to do: retreat to the campfire.

Instagram feels free because you never get an invoice. But you are not the customer, you are the inventory. Meta's average US and Canada user was worth $78.37 in 2025, eleven times the rest of the world. You sit well above that average, because Meta has tagged you as top-10-percent household income, a frequent international traveler, and an engaged shopper, the exact labels that make advertisers bid two to three times higher to reach you. At Meta's 41 percent operating margin, roughly four of every ten dollars your attention generates is pure profit. Over ten years that is likely $1,000 to $2,000, and it rises every year, even as you post less, because the affluence labels keep you premium whether you show up or not. This is not a scold. It is just the trade nobody names out loud, shown once so you can decide how you want to make it.

05 Two worlds

Your two networks

You run two social worlds that barely touch. Your 90 Instagram Close Friends are roughly 75 percent different people than your iMessage core. Instagram is your broader, younger, festival-and-creative graph. iMessage is chosen family and pro. Sociologists have a name for the position you occupy by instinct: you span a structural hole, the person who links two networks that would otherwise never meet. That is where introductions, opportunities, and reputation compound. It also means Instagram is uniquely valuable to you, because it is the only home for a whole slice of your people. Meta's map of your life is real but partial, weighted to the festival and creative side. Your inner ring lives in iMessage, outside their reach. That is the one part of your graph they do not own, and it is worth keeping that way on purpose.

08 The moves

What to actually do

Fifteen moves, grouped by category and effort, every one framed as a gain rather than a restriction. Start-here trio (all quick): turn off contact syncing and delete the 5,517 uploaded contacts, turn 'watch' into 'send' when a reel makes you think of someone, and prune the feed for joy. The rest range from keeping your inner ring in iMessage or Signal, to using Close Friends stories as your main broadcast, to a 90-second weekly glance at whether any apex-8 thread went quiet. 09 The horizon

Where this is all going

Public feeds, hollowing
43% of IG users haven't posted in 6+ months. AI slop rising. Performance, not connection.
Private campfires, growing ♥
DMs, close friends, group chats. DM shares now weighted 3-5x higher than likes. You already live here.

The internet is splitting in two. Public feeds are hollowing out under AI slop and ad load, while the real energy moves to private and small-group spaces, the digital campfires. You already live on the winning side. Instagram itself reorganized around exactly where you are: DMs, Reels, and recommendations, with DM shares now weighted three to five times higher than likes. Skip Threads and Bluesky; both are low-return text-performance platforms that host almost none of your people. One deadline worth a 20-minute audit: in July 2026 Meta removes the off-platform data opt-out, so do the audit and one last data download while the control still partly exists. In an internet getting more synthetic every quarter, a real human who actually shows up in your DMs is the scarce, appreciating asset. That is precisely what you are.
