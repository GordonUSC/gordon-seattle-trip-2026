# US Video Game Education & Scholars Pipeline · Starter Directory (Draft 3)
*Prepared by Gay Gaming Professionals · May 1, 2026*
*Built to be useful for **students** (filtered by age + region) AND for **donors** (with program type + efficacy specificity)*
*Rankings fact-checked against the Princeton Review 2026 Top Game Design lists on June 17, 2026.*

> **Framing.** This directory supports **all students pursuing careers in video games, including those from underestimated communities.** It uses the corporate language major industry partners use today (*belonging, culture, community responsibility, social impact, talent attraction*) instead of older identity-label vocabulary. **AI readiness for the rising generation** is treated as a first-class category alongside grade-level tiers. **It is built to be student-facing AND donor-facing.**

---

## How to read this directory

A **starter directory**, not a complete one. It captures the load-bearing programs, scholarships, and pipelines a student or family in the US should know about, sorted by **grade-level tier**, then **program type**, then **region**. Each entry has a one-line *what it is* + *efficacy / why a donor cares* tag.

The full version, post Take-Two Foundation launch partnership, will include 200 to 400 programs with verified outcomes data, hosted at **gaygamingpros.org/directory**, filterable, searchable, quarterly-refreshed.

### Two paths through this directory

**For a student or family**: filter by age (5-10 / 11-14 / 14-18 / 18-22 / 22+), region (West / Mountain / Midwest / South / Northeast / Texas / HBCU), cost tier (Free / sliding / tuition + scholarship / full pay), and audience served. The grade-level tiers below are the primary navigation.

**For a donor or funder**: every entry carries a *"where dollars go furthest"* lens. Outcomes data, cost-per-outcome, and pipeline-leakage points are surfaced in each tier's donor-lens callout. The framework at the back of this directory expands the funder view.

## SPECIAL CATEGORY · CIVIC × GAMES CROSSOVER

### IGNITE Impact Challenge · National Short-Form Video Competition

- **Prompt:** *Share Your Story* · how Public Law 119-21 (the One Big Beautiful Bill Act) is showing up in everyday life in your community.
- **Five categories:** SNAP & Medicaid · Student Loan · Immigration & Border · Energy & Climate · Individual or Business Tax.
- **Window:** April 10 – May 29, 2026.
- **Prize pool:** $50,000 ($5K / $3K / $2K per category × 5 categories = 15 winners).
- **Format:** smartphone-eligible. Solo or group. Short. Must be factual.
- **Audience:** 18+; specifically two- and four-year university students across 14 target departments (incl. Game Design).
- **Calendar:** mid-April portal live ✅ → summer judging → early September winner announcement.
- **Partners:** IGNITE National (Sara Guillermo CEO) · Games for Change (Susanna Pollack, Lucas Piloto) · GGP (Eric Francksen Executive Producer, Bec Godsey, Gordon Bellamy) · **Bigglesworth Family Foundation** (Sinjin Bain, sponsor).
- **Site:** [ignitenational.org/2026-impact-video-challenge](https://ignitenational.org/2026-impact-video-challenge)

**Why this entry matters in a games-education directory:** IGNITE proves that civic short-form video and game-design pedagogy can share a pipeline. Game designers are storytellers; this is storytelling-with-stakes.

## DIRECTORY USE-CASE FRAMEWORK

### For a student or family

> "I'm a [age] in [region]. What's available to me?"

The directory should be filterable by:
- **Age:** 5–10 / 11–14 / 14–18 / 18–22 / 22+
- **Region:** West / Mountain / Midwest / South / Northeast / Texas / HBCU
- **Cost tier:** Free / sliding scale / tuition + scholarship / full pay
- **Audience served:** Open / women+girls / underestimated communities / disability / first-gen / HBCU
- **Time-of-year:** Summer-only / year-round / academic-year-aligned

### For a donor / funder

> "I want to fund [outcome]. Where does my dollar go furthest?"

The directory should surface:
- **Outcomes data:** completion rate, downstream placement, industry hire rate
- **Cost-per-outcome:** $/student exposed, $/scholarship recipient, $/industry hire
- **Pipeline leakage points:** Where students drop out (typically: end of MS, end of HS, after 2nd year of college)
- **Match-making:** Funder's geographic/identity priorities → programs that already serve that population
- **Readiness layer:** which programs already include AI-tool fluency vs. which would benefit from an AI module overlay

### For GGP specifically

> "Where does GGP add unique value, distinct from existing programs?"

GGP's edge is **mentorship at scale plus industry visibility for all students pursuing careers in games, including those from underestimated communities.** Twenty years of network compounds into placements; the 2K Studio Tour at GDC tradition compounds into trust; the Collegiate Games Challenge compounds into discovery. The directory positions GGP not as a competitor but as the **connective tissue** across programs · a published, maintained resource that donors fund and students depend on.

## What this directory needs to become real

1. **Verified outcomes data** for each entry (completion rate, placement rate, hire rate). Currently most programs do not publish this. Procuring it is a research lift the proposal funds.
2. **Real-time updates** · at least quarterly. Scholarship deadlines, new programs, defunct programs.
3. **Web-hosted, filterable, searchable** · not a PDF. Hosted at gaygamingpros.org/directory.
4. **Editorial standards** · what gets in, what doesn't, how new entries are vetted. (Recommend: industry-press coverage + outcomes data + audience-served alignment.)
5. **Donor-facing dashboard** · separate view aggregating gaps and impact opportunities.
6. **AI-readiness overlay** · every entry tagged for AI-curriculum status; the gap map becomes a fundable scope of work.
