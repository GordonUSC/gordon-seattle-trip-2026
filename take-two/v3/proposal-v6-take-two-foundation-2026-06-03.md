## Why now

Twenty years of GGP. **Six years of partnership with Take-Two, every year since 2019, the most consistent corporate partnership in our history.** The CYSTEM Mentorship Program you and I formalized in 2023 is still placing students into careers, including the **first GGP Scholar Alum named to Forbes 30 Under 30 (September 2025).** The annual **2K Studio Tour at GDC** has become tradition. You personally participated again at GDC 2026, and your presence at the **GGP Scholars Breakfast** that Christopher Kim coordinated last year was felt across the whole cohort. The "EXCELLENCE" cadence keeps working.

**This year, our shared work has a new structural moment.** The **US Video Game Education and Scholars Pipeline Directory** is ready. A board-ready map of the field, from elementary through graduate, with AI readiness as a first-class category, with **"where dollars go furthest"** tags on every entry. It is the artifact your board has been waiting for: navigable, donor-facing, student-facing, and built to be quarterly-refreshed at **gaygamingpros.org/directory.** **Take-Two as the named launch partner of the Directory makes it the structural anchor of the games-education field.** That is bigger than any single year of programming. It positions Take-Two as anchoring the next generation of games-education infrastructure for everyone, not just the cohort we directly serve.

Now the talent pipeline also needs us at our best. The students we are mentoring this year are graduating into the hardest entry-level market in two decades. **AI readiness** has become the new threshold question for talent attraction in the games industry. Take-Two's own April 2026 recalibration of its AI strategy is a public signal of where the puck is going. And our shared work, supporting *all students pursuing careers in games, including those from underestimated communities*, has never been more relevant to Take-Two's culture and to the next generation.

This document proposes **three options** for renewing our partnership for the **next 12 months (July 2026 to July 2027).** All three slot inside an existing partnership budget structure. All three are framed as continuations of work we already do well together, **with the Directory as the new spine.** **None require approvals beyond the corporate communications and partnerships function.**

## Option A · Directory Launch · $40,000

**$40,000 · 12-month engagement · Pride 2026 launch**

The Directory exists in draft. Option A funds its public launch as a Take-Two-anchored resource.

**What it delivers:**
- Public web launch of the US Video Game Education and Scholars Pipeline at **gaygamingpros.org/directory**
- Filterable + searchable interface (by age, region, cost, audience served, AI-readiness)
- **"Powered by Take-Two Interactive, launch partner"** attribution prominently displayed
- **200+ programs** in the launch directory with verified scope data
- Quarterly refresh cycle for 12 months
- Annual report on directory usage with downloadable cuts for funders and educators

**Where it shows up:**
- gaygamingpros.org/directory (primary)
- Cross-posted to GGP YouTube channel for launch announcement
- Press cycle at Pride 2026 launch: GamesBeat, PocketGamer.biz, Polygon, EdSurge
- Annual report at end-of-year showing reach and engagement

**What we own:** directory build, hosting, editorial standards, quarterly refresh, press
**What Take-Two owns:** launch-partner brand approvals, launch comms participation

**Honest assessment:** Option A is the structural-anchor play. It positions Take-Two as the partner who made navigation of the games-education field possible. It does *not* extend the **CYSTEM Mentorship**, the **2K Studio Tour**, the **Scholars Breakfast**, or fund the **AI readiness** module. Several pieces of our existing partnership are left on the table.

## Option B · Directory + Pipeline + AI ⭐
### Our recommended choice

**$100,000 · 12-month engagement · spans Pride 2026 to GDC 2027 to Pride 2027**

Option B leads with the Directory as the public good, continues every structural piece of our existing partnership, and adds the AI readiness layer.

**What it delivers:**

### B.1 · Directory Launch + Year-1 Maintenance ($50,000)
Everything in Option A, plus:
- **Donor-facing dashboard:** gap maps, cost-per-outcome, "where dollars go furthest" surfaced as a funder lens
- **Verified outcomes data** for the top 50 programs (research procurement is the work this funds)
- **AI-readiness overlay** tagging every entry: which programs already include AI-tool fluency, which would benefit from an AI module overlay
- **Expanded launch scope** to ~250 programs with deeper data

### B.2 · Named Take-Two CYSTEM Cohort ($30,000)
A 12-month cohort of **10 scholars** from across the country, supporting all students pursuing careers in games, including those from underestimated communities. **CYSTEM Year 4** under Alex Rogers's continued leadership. Take-Two named on the cohort; scholarship dollars stacked from existing GGP CYSTEM funder mix to reach full per-scholar amounts.

- $2,000 Take-Two anchor portion per scholar × 10 = $20,000 (stacked with CYSTEM co-funders to full scholarship amount)
- $1,000 GDC 2027 travel stipend × 10 = $10,000
- 1-on-1 ERG mentor pairings (each scholar matched with a Take-Two ERG member)
- 6 mentor sessions over the year, GGP-curated

### B.3 · Annual 2K Studio Tour + Scholars Breakfast, Take-Two as named industry sponsor of GDC 2027 ($15,000)
- Underwrites the **2K Studio Tour** for the cohort and the **GGP Scholars Breakfast** at GDC 2027 in San Francisco
- Both are existing traditions Alan personally values and participates in
- Take-Two named as the industry sponsor, visible at the event, in press, and in our annual report

### B.4 · AI Readiness Track for the Rising Generation ($5,000)
Brand-new addition. Embedded in CYSTEM and the Directory.

- "AI in the Studio" curriculum module added to CYSTEM Mentorship
- 1 to 2 AI fluency office hours led by Take-Two engineers
- "AI in the Studio" segment within the 2K Studio Tour at GDC 2027
- The Directory's AI-readiness overlay feeds back into this work

**Outcomes (12-month metrics):**
- Directory: live at gaygamingpros.org/directory by Pride 2026; **5,000+ unique users in year 1**; 250+ programs at launch; quarterly refresh hit on schedule
- Press: 1,500+ press impressions across the Pride 2026 launch cycle
- Cohort: 10 scholars deployed, 80% mentor-pairing completion, at least 2 cohort members hired into industry roles by July 2027
- 2K Tour + Breakfast: 45+ scholars in attendance at GDC 2027 (matches 2026 baseline)
- AI track: 100% of cohort completes the module; at least 3 case-study reflections published
- Relationship: renewable into Option C for 2027 to 2028 if all four land

**What we own:** directory build + maintenance, scholarship deployment, mentor curation, AI curriculum, GDC programming, public storytelling
**What Take-Two owns:** launch-partner brand presence, ERG access for mentor pool, AI engineer access (1 to 2 sessions), brand approvals, breakfast presence

## Option C · Multi-Year Flagship

**$250,000 over 24 months (July 2026 to July 2028) · $125,000 per year average**

Option C is presented if Take-Two wants to make GGP × Take-Two a named multi-year flagship anchored on the Directory.

**What it would add on top of Option B:**
- **Year 2 mirroring** of the cohort + Studio Tour + Breakfast
- **Directory expanded to 400+ programs** with full verified outcomes data, hosted under prominent co-branding: **"Powered by Take-Two Interactive"** anchor positioning
- **A New York-based mirror of the GGP × G4C charity poker tournament** (the inaugural one, hosted by Mike + Amy Morhaime, just funded two GGP/G4C Scholars for 2026 to 2027; appetite to mirror)
- *Optional add-on:* a second "Belonging Round 3" video series in 2027 if Take-Two wants the dual-asset package (~$30K incremental, not included in Option C base)

**Honest assessment:** Option C is a real commitment, $125,000 per year for two years. It would make GGP × Take-Two the most visible games-education infrastructure partnership in the industry at this scale tier. We are presenting it because some of our peer organizations have suggested Take-Two might want this scale of visibility around major 2027 launches. We respect Take-Two's discipline on commitments and are not pushing Option C unless the bandwidth is there.

## Why Option B is right for July 2026 to July 2027

- **At $100,000, it is the best year of the partnership in dollar terms while still inside Alan's existing partnership budget envelope**, no escalation required
- **The Directory makes Take-Two's name structural in the field**, not just a single-year asset
- **It renews three traditions Take-Two already participates in** (CYSTEM mentorship, 2K Studio Tour, Scholars Breakfast)
- **It adds AI readiness**, the talent-pipeline question every games company is currently navigating, including Take-Two
- **It costs less than a single tier-2 GDC sponsorship line item** but produces compounding pipeline value across 12 months
- **At $10,000 per direct beneficiary** (10 scholars), it is well below industry benchmarks for entry-level pipeline ROI
- **It positions Take-Two as the industry's most consistent partner** in supporting the rising generation, exactly when consistency is rare
- **The Sinjin / Bigglesworth Family Foundation parallel partnership** (the *T2 2025 Support* thread you and Sinjin opened, plus the Bigglesworth-sponsored IGNITE Impact Challenge in cycle now) means GGP is already sitting at the connective tissue between two of Take-Two's natural funder peers, a structural advantage for any multi-year flagship

## Risk + mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Take-Two budget constraint in 2026 | Option A is the fallback; Option B is structured so each component can be modular if needed |
| Directory launch delays | Pride 2026 launch decoupled from GDC 2027 deliverables; phased release acceptable (beta → public) |
| Mentor matching at scale | GGP pre-recruits 15+ Take-Two ERG members for the pool; mentor handbook + monthly office hours |
| AI track scope creep | Curriculum is delivered by GGP partners; Take-Two provides 1 to 2 engineer office hours, not full curriculum ownership |
| Directory outcomes data not available for all programs | Top 50 verified at launch; longer-tail entries flagged as "scope only"; outcomes filled in across the year |
| Conflicts with Take-Two's existing ERG load | All asks scoped to 6 hours or less per ERG member per year |
