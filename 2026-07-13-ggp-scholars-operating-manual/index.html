02 · The person

A scholar's journey, end to end

STAGE 1

Apply

Jun-Jul

One Kaleidoscope application, routed by fit to a track. Chosen name and pronouns honored from field one. Craft, values, community service, and an AI-empowerment prompt. No cost to apply; potential counts as much as polish.

STAGE 2

Select

Jul

AI helps organize and surface, a human decides every scholar. Reviewers score against a rubric; you give final sign-off. Elite by depth, never by scarcity, we do not publish how many we decline.

STAGE 3

Onboard

Aug

Placed in a triangle buddy group (three scholars across schools) and paired with a 1:1 industry mentor. Given their year map, their capstone brief, and a named-fund welcome if a donor carries their scholarship.

STAGE 4

The Scholar year

Sep-Feb

Monthly workshops, the AI-in-the-Studio module, mentor check-ins, a community-service commitment, and a capstone they build across the year. Alex's mentorship engine carries this. Wellbeing and belonging are tracked, not just deliverables.

STAGE 5

Capstone at GDC

Mar

The finale every track converges on: GDC All-Access, the Scholars Breakfast, the 2K studio tour (Take-Two in-kind), and a showcase of AI-empowered capstones the industry sees. This is the visible apex of the Directory.

STAGE 6

Fellow

Year 2, selective

A second-year deeper build plus a paid opportunity or placement. This tier is what signals a career, not a certificate, and it is the outcome funders renew for.

STAGE 7

Mentor

Lifelong

Alumni return to mentor the next cohort. The give-back is membership in a guild, not a transaction, and it is how the network compounds year over year.

04 · The engine

How mentorship actually works

The triangle buddy group

Three scholars from three schools, paired as peers for the year. It is CYSTEM's proven core, and it is what makes a large program feel intimate. Peers catch what mentors miss, and cross-school ties become the alumni network later.

  • Formed at onboarding, holds all year
  • Meets on its own cadence, plus program checkpoints
  • The unit of belonging, and the reason scale does not turn cold

The 1:1 industry mentor

Each scholar matched to a working professional. Mentors come from your network and the sponsor coalition (a corporate sponsorship can include mentoring seats for its employees, which is a feature Alan explicitly asked for).

  • Matched at onboarding by Alex, approved by a human
  • Regular check-ins across the year
  • Guides the capstone and opens real doors
Why this survives the merge intact: the mentorship model is the one thing every voice in the roundtable agreed we cannot lose. It stays exactly as CYSTEM ran it, now extended to all four tracks and fed by a wider mentor pool.
05 · The differentiator

AI empowerment, as curriculum

This is what makes GGP leaders in AI empowerment rather than a program that mentions AI. It is taught as amplification with a human always in the loop, the way you work with Darby.

The "AI in the Studio" module

  • Co-built with Take-Two, core curriculum, not an elective
  • Hands-on: each scholar directs AI on a real piece of their craft
  • Ethics + human-in-the-loop taught as first principles, not footnotes

The AI-empowered capstone

  • Every scholar ships one project that uses AI to amplify their work
  • Shown at the GDC showcase, the visible proof of the promise
  • GGP publishes what it learns, so the field, not just the cohort, benefits
Positioning: in the Directory, "AI readiness" is a first-class category. GGP's program is the one that does not just rank AI readiness, it produces it. That is the apex claim.
06 · The fuel

How it gets funded

Named funds · personal foundations

A donor names a scholarship (Carter Lipscomb is the first). High touch: meet-your-scholar, an annual letter in the scholar's words, a multi-year bond. Intimacy is the product.

  • Values guardrail: a named fund honors GGP's values; no condition narrows who is welcome
  • Joe papers the fund agreement; Darby runs the stewardship cadence
  • Your job: cultivate the donor and make the human introduction

Sponsorships · corporations

Take-Two anchors at $80K (CYSTEM $25K + GGP $25K + Directory $30K, plus the 2K tour in-kind). Tiers scale from there: named cohort, track sponsor, AI-module underwriter, mentoring seats.

  • Recognition at the apex of the Directory; coalition, not exclusivity
  • Renews on an outcomes dashboard: placements, projects, Fellows made
  • The Houser gap (~$90K) closes through the EA + ESA coalition Alan invited
07 · Your lane

Your responsibilities, precisely

Seven jobs. The ones marked CORE are your non-substitutable value, the seams only you can broker. The rest you set in motion and then hold, you do not run them.

1

Guard the vision and the values

Core

You own what "elite by outcome, not exclusion" means and you hold the language: underestimated, belonging, coalition, never scarcity. When a funder or a well-meaning teammate drifts toward gatekeeping or corporate coldness, you are the one who pulls it back.

Does: approve the program identity and name; keep the North Star intact; settle values questions.

2

Own the funder relationships and the raise

Core

This is your highest-leverage work, the part no one else can do. Renew Take-Two with Alan. Build the EA and ESA coalition (Stanley) to close the Houser gap. Cultivate personal foundations and land named funds (Sussanah and her peers). Give Sinjin a real home as he rolls into GGP.

Does: the relationship calls, the asks, the coalition brokering. Joe papers it; Darby preps the decks and tracks the pipeline.

3

Recruit the top of the funnel

Core

Your platform and voice bring in scholars, and your network brings in the 1:1 industry mentors and studio partners that make the year real. You open doors; Alex matches and runs them.

Does: the megaphone, the introductions, the "yes" from a mentor or a studio only you can get.

4

Steward the naming and legacy layer

Core

The Carter Lipscomb memorial strand is yours to shepherd, with Phil Rosenberg and Carter's circle. Named funds carry emotional weight; you are the one who holds that with the families and honors the people behind the names.

Does: the Carter fund; the human side of every named gift. Not the paperwork.

5

Hold the apex positioning

Core

The Directory and your industry standing are what make GGP perceptibly elite. Your USC faculty seat, your public voice, and your presence at GDC keep GGP at the top of the field's own map.

Does: the public face, the keynote, the Directory as field infrastructure. Darby builds and maintains it.

6

Give final selection sign-off

A human decides every scholar, and the last human is you. Reviewers and the rubric do the triage; you make the judgment call on the cohort, especially the values and trajectory reads a rubric cannot capture.

Does: approve the final list. You do not read every application cold, that is the review team.

Empower Alex (mentorship), Eric (production), and hold the coalition of people together. A monthly rhythm with your leads, not daily operations. Your presence sets the culture; their hands run the machine.

Does: monthly leads check-in, unblock, set tone. Weekly Connect runs with or without you.

08 · The discipline

What you should hand off

Your metacognitive profile is clear on this: you are the graph, not the nodes. These are real, necessary jobs, and every one of them belongs to someone other than you. Protect your time for the seams only you can broker.

Delegate, do not do

  • Application operations and cohort tracking → Darby + the review team. You sign off; you do not administer.
  • Mentor scheduling and the weekly cadence → Alex. You recruit the mentor; Alex runs the relationship.
  • The calendar, Weekly Connect, cross-track logistics → Eric as Executive Producer.
  • Funder paperwork, fund agreements, transfers → Joe and Isabella. You make the ask; they close the mechanics.
  • Dashboards, outcome reports, the Directory build → Darby. You get a one-page brief, not a spreadsheet.
  • Chasing status → the back office surfaces what needs you; you do not go looking for it.
The test for any task: if someone else in the coalition could do it with a warm introduction from you, hand it off with that introduction. If it dies without you specifically in the room, that is your job. Almost everything that only you can do is a relationship, a value, or a name.
