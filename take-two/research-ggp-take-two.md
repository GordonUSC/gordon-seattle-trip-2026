Therefore the right shape is **a partnership renewal proposal, not a grant application.** It must slot inside Alan Lewis's existing partnership budget so he can approve at his level without escalating.

## 3. GGP's full program portfolio (Take-Two should see this)

GGP has been active for **20 years**, since 2006, supporting students pursuing careers in video games — including those from underestimated communities. Programs:

### 3.1 GGP Scholars (flagship, 7 years running · launched ~2019)
- The original GGP scholarship program. Identifies and cultivates emerging top-performer students through education, mentorship, and industry placement.
- Alumni include:
  - **Audrey Webb** — first-class GGP Scholar, now Product Designer at Roblox
  - **Joseph Kemp** — first GGP Scholar Alum on Forbes 30 Under 30 (Games, Sept 2025); CEO Games That Matter LLC; creator of *Disbarred*; Penn MBA/JD + Michigan Ross
  - Active GGP Scholar exemplar: **Allison Lee** (interested in The Sims, Innersloth, LA indie culture)
- Network is the asset. Two decades of placements compound.

### 3.2 CYSTEM Scholars (formalized 2022-2023)
- Mentorship-anchored scholarship program. Each scholar matched with an industry mentor.
- Take-Two-anchored since 2023 via the CYSTEM Mentorship Program above.
- Standout alums include **Ami Cai** at NYS Empire State Development, **Christopher Kim** (USC MBA, Marshall School of Business president).

### 3.3 Collegiate Games Challenge (CGC) — launched 2025
- National competition where universities (USC Games, USC Marshall, DePaul Jarvis CDM, others) nominate two student game projects + students develop trailers and go-to-market strategies for cash prizes.
- **$90,000 in prizes** awarded inaugural year (livestream August 13, 2025)
- Partner: **Raw Thrills** (arcade entertainment company)
- Judges from: Roblox (Audrey Webb), industry network
- 2026 CGC lottery already running (Jan 2026 communication)
- [Sources: GamesBeat, GamePress, collegiategameschallenge.com](https://collegiategameschallenge.com/rules)

### 3.4 GGP at Global Game Jam (third year running)
- GGP hosts an official online/virtual Global Game Jam site, accessible to all (not just scholars cohort)
- 2026 jam hosted on the Gay Gaming Professionals Discord
- This year GGJ is partnering with Games for Change to launch **GGJ Next** (ages 10–18) and **G4C-GGJ Next Gen Expert Jam** (ages 18–25). GGP sits at the connective tissue.

### 3.5 GGP × Games for Change collaboration (2026 — newly funded)
- Two GGP Scholars in 2026–2027 are funded by a **collaborative fundraising effort** — the inaugural charity poker tournament hosted by **Mike and Amy Morhaime** (Dreamhaven; Amy is a co-founder/board member of Games for Change). Potential mirror in New York.
- **Susanna Pollack** (G4C President) and Gordon are collaborators on this and on the IGNITE Impact Challenge.
- This is the live precedent for matched fundraising at scale that Take-Two could anchor as the next industry partner.

### 3.6 Other partner programs in GGP's orbit
- **ESA Foundation × GGP Scholarship Fund** (joint Donorbox fund)
- **Dignitas GGP Scholarship Program** (esports)
- **Kaleidoscope GGP Grant** for Game Development, Esports, and Content

## 7. AI readiness — the new add-on this proposal introduces

Gordon's 2026 emphasis: **AI readiness for the rising generation.**

Take-Two has a stake here. The April 2026 layoff of Take-Two's head of AI (per Game Developer Daily) is a public signal that the company is recalibrating, not abandoning, its AI strategy. A GGP-led AI readiness curriculum for scholars would land at exactly the moment Take-Two is figuring out what comes next on the talent side.

Concrete proposal pieces (detailed in the proposal doc):
- AI literacy module added to CYSTEM Mentorship curriculum
- "AI in the Studio" panel as part of the 2K Studio Tour at GDC 2027
- Take-Two ERG mentors paired with scholars on AI-tool fluency

This is presented as a **forward-looking talent-pipeline differentiator**, not a politicized topic.
