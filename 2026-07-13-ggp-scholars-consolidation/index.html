One Scholars Program.
Four ways in.

Four scholar efforts, built at different times for different funders, become a single program with one front door, one mentorship spine, one GDC-week finale, and one story funders can rally behind.

Private working draft · July 13, 2026 · every number carries a receipt from the primary record
Trigger: Houser Foundation is out Anchor stays: Take-Two $80K GGP turns 20 in 2026 First Forbes 30U30 alum: Joseph Kemp

Why consolidate now

The old structure ran four separate efforts chasing four separate funders. That worked while the money was distributed. It stops working the moment an anchor leaves. Dan Houser (Houser Foundation, roughly $90K to CYSTEM) is no longer supporting, and Sinjin Bain is stepping back from CYSTEM. Rather than let CYSTEM quietly fade, we fold its strongest parts, and three other scholar efforts, into one durable program with a shared funder base. One program is easier to fund, easier to run, and tells a bigger story than four.

What consolidation wins

  • Fundable. One program with one story, easier to underwrite than four scattered lines.
  • Durable. No single funder's exit can collapse a whole track anymore.
  • Simpler to run. One application, one mentor pool, one calendar, one lead structure.
  • Bigger for scholars. A CYSTEM year flows straight into the GDC-week experience.
  • A place for Sinjin. Rolled into GGP, potentially reviving the Collegiate Games Challenge.
  • Honors Carter. A living memorial strand, not a one-time tribute.
